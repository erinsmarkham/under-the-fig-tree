# Setting Up Your Website on GitHub — A Step-by-Step Guide

Hi! This guide will walk you through three things:
1. Creating your free GitHub account
2. Creating a "repository" (a folder) for your website
3. Adding your friend as a collaborator so they can upload your website code
4. Turning on free website hosting (GitHub Pages)

It looks long, but each step is just a click or two. You'll be done in about 15 minutes. Take your time!

---

## What is GitHub?

Think of GitHub like Google Drive, but specifically for website code. It stores your website files online, lets you share them with collaborators, and can even host the website itself for free.

---

## Part 1: Create Your GitHub Account (5 minutes)

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge, etc.)
2. Go to **https://github.com**
3. Click the green **"Sign up"** button in the top-right corner
4. Enter your **email address** and click **Continue**
5. Create a **password** (write it down somewhere safe!) and click **Continue**
6. Pick a **username** — this will be public, so choose something you're comfortable with (like your name or your business name). Click **Continue**
7. Answer the verification puzzle if it shows one
8. Click **Create account**
9. GitHub will email you a code. Open your email, find the message from GitHub, and copy the code into the website
10. You can skip any "personalize your experience" questions by clicking **Skip personalization** at the bottom
11. When asked to pick a plan, choose the **Free** plan

You now have a GitHub account! Make a note of your **username** — you'll need it.

---

## Part 2: Create a Repository for Your Website (3 minutes)

A "repository" (or "repo") is just a folder that holds your website files.

1. After signing in, look at the **top-right corner** of the page. You'll see a **"+"** icon. Click it.
2. From the menu, click **"New repository"**
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Repository name**: Pick a simple name like `my-website` or `under-the-fig-tree` (no spaces — use dashes instead)
   - **Description**: Optional. You can write something like "My website"
   - **Public vs Private**: Choose **Public** ← This is important! GitHub's free website hosting only works with public repositories. (Public means anyone can *see* the code, but only people you invite can *change* it.)
   - **Initialize this repository with**: Check the box for **"Add a README file"**
4. Click the green **"Create repository"** button at the bottom

Your repository is now ready!

---

## Part 3: Add Your Friend as a Collaborator (3 minutes)

This gives your friend permission to upload and update your website files.

1. You should be on your repository's page now. If not, click your profile picture (top-right), choose **"Your repositories"**, and click on the repo you just made.
2. Near the top of the page, look for a row of tabs that says: `Code  Issues  Pull requests  Actions  Projects  Wiki  Security  Insights  Settings`
3. Click **"Settings"** (it's on the far right — you may need to scroll the tab row)
4. On the **left sidebar**, look for **"Collaborators"** under the "Access" section, and click it
5. GitHub may ask you to confirm your password — type it in
6. Click the green **"Add people"** button
7. In the search box, type your **friend's GitHub username** (ask them what it is!)
8. Click on their name when it appears
9. Click **"Add [username] to this repository"**
10. Your friend will get an email invitation. Once they accept it, they can push code to your repo.

---

## Part 4: Turn On Free Website Hosting (2 minutes)

This makes your website live on the internet at a free URL.

1. Still in your repository, click the **"Settings"** tab again (top of the page)
2. On the **left sidebar**, scroll down and click **"Pages"** (under the "Code and automation" section)
3. Under **"Build and deployment"**:
   - **Source**: Make sure it says **"Deploy from a branch"**
   - **Branch**: Click the dropdown that says "None" and select **"main"**. Leave the folder dropdown as **"/ (root)"**
4. Click **"Save"**
5. Wait about 1–2 minutes, then refresh the page
6. At the top of the Pages settings, you'll see a green box with your website's URL — something like `https://yourusername.github.io/my-website/`
7. That's your live website! It might be empty until your friend uploads the code.

---

## What to Send Your Friend

Once you've finished the steps above, send your friend a message with:
1. Your **GitHub username**
2. The **repository name** you picked
3. A note that you've **invited them as a collaborator** (they'll need to accept the email invitation)

That's everything they need to upload your website code!

---

## If You Get Stuck

- **Lost in settings?** Click the repository name at the top of the page to go back to the main view, then try again.
- **Can't find your friend's username?** Just ask them — it's the name they used when they signed up for GitHub.
- **Invitation didn't arrive?** Have them check their spam folder, or you can copy the invitation link from the Collaborators page and send it directly.
- **Website not showing up?** GitHub Pages can take 5–10 minutes the first time. Be patient!

You've got this!
